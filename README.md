## 💰 What you call Money

 * **The Fiat Creation:** The central bank then "buys" these bonds. But they don't buy them with existing money; they simply type numbers into a computer to credit the government's account.

### 2. Derivatives, Futures, and Collateral (The House of Cards)
Once the fiat is created, the traditional financial system builds towering, leveraged structures on top of it.
 * **Futures Contracts:** Originally, these were honest agreements for farmers to lock in a price for next season's wheat. Today, they are highly leveraged financial contracts betting on the future price of an asset, often settled in cash without the physical asset ever changing hands.
 * **Derivatives:** These are financial instruments that derive their value from an underlying asset, like a stock, a mortgage, or a bond. They are essentially bets placed on the performance of other assets.
 * **The Collateral Problem:** To make these bets, institutions pledge collateral. The fatal flaw of the honest-looking system is "rehypothecation." This means the exact same piece of collateral is pledged multiple times by different parties to back multiple different derivatives. There are infinitely more paper claims to assets than there are physical assets to back them up.
### 3. The Trap of Third-Party Counterparty Risk
This is usually the hardest pill for people to swallow: when you deposit fiat into a bank, it legally ceases to be yours.
